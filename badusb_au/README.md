# BadUSB / HID Attacks (Australian Focus)

BadUSB remains one of the highest success rate vectors when used with social engineering in Australia.

## Why It's Strong Here
- Australians still click shit they shouldn't.
- Many companies and tradies still run Windows with poor endpoint protection.
- Government, banks, and "official looking" lures work extremely well.

## Recommended AU-Themed Payload Ideas (for authorized testing only)
- Fake "MyGov" or "ATO" update prompts
- Fake NBN / Telstra / Optus technician scripts
- "Invoice attached - urgent" payloads targeting tradies
- Fake AusPost / parcel delivery executables
- "Windows Update for AU English language pack" lures

## Good Tools
- Flipper itself (basic)
- Hak5 Bash Bunny / O.MG cable for more serious work
- Custom PowerShell / DuckyScript tuned for AU environments

## Rules
- This folder is for research and authorized red team / physical assessment work only.
- Never use on systems you do not have explicit permission to test.
- Real social engineering + good pretext beats fancy payloads 9 times out of 10 in Australia.

Populate with working (tested) AU-specific payloads and notes only.
