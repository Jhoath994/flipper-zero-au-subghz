# Common iButton Readers in Australia (Field Notes)

This is real-world data from Australian tradies, depots, rural properties, and workshops as of 2026.

## Very Common Readers

### DS9097 / DS9097E (Maxim/Dallas)
- Classic. Still everywhere on older trucks and gates.
- Flipper reads and emulates these extremely well.
- Most "universal" Chinese iButton copiers target this family.

### RW1990 / TM1990 Compatible Writers
- The cheap blue Chinese programmers sold on eBay AU and AliExpress.
- They write the 64-bit ID directly.
- Flipper can do the exact same thing (and verify it).

### ATA / B&D iButton Readers (Gate Systems)
- Very common on Australian roller doors and driveway gates (especially 2005-2018 installs).
- Many still use plain DS1990A style iButtons for "master" or "technician" access.
- Huge win for rural and industrial properties.

### Fleet / Truck Immobiliser Readers
- Common on Isuzu, Hino, older Kenworth, Volvo, and many Australian fleet trucks.
- Often mounted on the dash or door pillar.
- Frequently just simple ID match (not challenge-response).

### Tool Crib / Workshop Drawers
- Many Australian workshops and mining sites still use iButton for tool tracking.
- Often the cheapest possible readers.

## What Still Works Well (2026)
- Simple DS1990A / RW1990 clones → 80-90% success rate on older systems.
- Many rural properties and older depots have not upgraded.
- Chinese utes and machinery often use very basic iButton implementations.

## What is Harder
- Newer "encrypted" or rolling iButton systems (rare but increasing in mining/fleet).
- Some modern access control panels that combine iButton + PIN or RFID.

## Recommended Blanks to Carry (Australia)
1. **RW1990** (blue, cheap, widely available on eBay AU)
2. **DS1990A** clones
3. **TM1990** (more expensive but better quality)

Buy a pack of 10 RW1990s and keep them on you. They cost almost nothing.
